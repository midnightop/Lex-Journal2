Constitutional

The Constitution of India

The supreme law of India. It establishes the framework for fundamental political principles, procedures, and duties. It is the bedrock of Indian democracy and the longest written constitution in the world.

Fact: Contains 448 Articles in 25 Parts.
Criminal

Indian Penal Code (IPC)

The official criminal code of India. It defines crimes and provides punishments for the entire country. Note: IPC is currently being replaced by the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS).

Landmark Judgments

Decisions that reshaped the Indian legal landscape.

Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala

1973 • Supreme Court

"The Parliament has the power to amend the Constitution, but it cannot alter its Basic Structure."

This landmark case introduced the 'Basic Structure Doctrine', ensuring that the core identity of the Constitution remains protected from political changes.
