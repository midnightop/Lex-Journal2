Bare Acts Database

Constitution of India, 1950

The supreme law of India. It lays down the framework defining fundamental political principles, establishes the structure, procedures, powers, and duties of government institutions and sets out fundamental rights, directive principles and the duties of citizens.

Indian Penal Code, 1860

The official criminal code of India. It is a comprehensive code intended to cover all substantive aspects of criminal law. Note: Currently being replaced by Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS).

Indian Evidence Act, 1872

Governs the admissibility of evidence in Indian courts. It defines what facts are relevant and how they can be proved in a court of law.

Landmark Judgments

Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala (1973)

Issue: Can Parliament amend any part of the Constitution?

Verdict: Introduced the 'Basic Structure Doctrine'. Parliament cannot alter the essential features of the Constitution.

Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India (1978)

Issue: Right to travel abroad under Article 21.

Verdict: Expanded 'Right to Life' to include 'Right to Live with Dignity'. Law must be just, fair, and reasonable.
